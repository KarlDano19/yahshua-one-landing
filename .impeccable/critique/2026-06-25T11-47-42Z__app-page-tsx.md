## Anti-Patterns Verdict

Does this look AI-generated? Yes, clearly. The page fails the first-order slop check without needing a second look. Five specific tells fire: cream background (#FAFAF7), steel blue accent (oklch(0.74 0.12 215)), gradient text ban on hero, eyebrow on every section (6 instances), numbered module markers (01-05). The Intelligence section (dark ink panel) is the one section that escapes the template.

Deterministic scan: One finding — layout-transition at page.tsx:627. FALSE POSITIVE: the transition only covers border-color and box-shadow; height: "100%" at that line is a static layout property, not animated.

## Overall Impression

The copy is excellent — specific, Filipino-market-aware, honest. The AI showcase section is the page's strongest moment. But the visual identity is a generic SaaS template wearing a Filipino name. The biggest opportunity: commit to a color strategy that doesn't share a palette with every US SaaS tool.

## What's Working

## Priority Issues

[P0] Gradient text on hero — WebkitBackgroundClip: 'text' + gradient on "runs on." (page.tsx:468). Absolute ban. Fix: solid color var(--accent-2).

[P0] No responsive layout — 12-column module grid, 4-column figures, 2-column AI showcase, 5-column footer all use static inline styles with no media queries. Broken on mobile for the primary Filipino audience.

[P1] Brand identity crisis — cream + steel blue = textbook Stripe-adjacent SaaS, directly contradicting anti-references. Color strategy decision needed.

[P1] Eyebrow on every section — 6 instances of <Dot /> [Section Name] in mono uppercase tracked text (page.tsx:524, 666, 772, 829, 956, 1019). Most visible AI scaffold pattern.

[P1] "Watch the tour" goes nowhere — links to empty div#platform anchor. ProductSidebar and ProductMain defined (lines 80-320) but never rendered.

[P2] Numbered module markers — 01·ERP through 05·Personal as scaffolding, not meaningful sequence.

[P2] Figures section is the hero-metric template — big numbers, small labels, 4-column grid.
